Southern Australia

Adelaide

Winter crop production in South Australia is estimated to have fallen by 2% in 2021–22 to 8.4 million tonnes, but remains 16% above the 10-

year average to 2020–21.

Seasonal conditions in South Australia were variable during the winter cropping season. Production in southern cropping regions is

estimated to be generally well above average, supported by above average rainfall and cool conditions in late spring. However, production

prospects in northern cropping regions were damaged by dry conditions in August and September, and those earlier finishing areas were

generally unable to benefit from the more favourable conditions in late spring.
